Welcome to the Digital Edition of Capitano Ulisse

Digital Edition Overview

This website presents a digital critical edition of Capitano Ulisse, a theatrical work characterised by a complex process of composition and revision. In the original manuscript, the title appears as Capitan Ulisse, later standardised as Capitano Ulisse from the first printed edition onward.

The edition offers two complementary entry points: a genetic manuscript view and a stabilised printed text.

Manuscript Visualisation

The manuscript view allows users to explore the play as it evolved through successive layers of authorial correction. These layers, corresponding to distinct stages of revision, are referred to as witnesses and can be selected through a dropdown menu.

  • What you see: variants displayed ...
  • Highlighting: changes are visually emphasised when a witness diverges from the base layer.
  • Fallback: if a layer contains no change, the previous stage is shown to preserve continuity.

Encoding approach

The transcription is encoded in TEI (Text Encoding Initiative) and adopts a parallel segmentation model to represent seven distinct layers of authorial intervention.

How to Use the Interface

The viewer synchronises transcribed text with manuscript facsimiles and contextual metadata.

  1. Page selection Navigate page by page; each page aligns text and the corresponding facsimile image.
  2. Witness selection (manuscript view only) Switch between layers of revision; divergences are highlighted against the base layer.
  3. Facsimile synchronisation Compare the documentary source directly with its encoded representation.
  4. Metadata panel Open “Show / hide metadata” to access bibliographic info, related resources, and page-level named entities.

Through this interface, users can move between genetic analysis, documentary evidence, and contextual metadata—supporting both close study and broader exploration.
